# Research Plan: Leading Through Change: Emotional Agility at Horizon Media

## Task-to-Evidence Matrix

| Task section | What must be answered | Evidence to collect | Suggested artifact |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Emotional Agility Analysis | Identify specific emotional hooks and thought patterns affecting her leadership Evaluate how her relationship with difficult emotions impacts her effectiveness Analyze the role of values and identity in her leadership transition | Academic theory, public company or case evidence, and relevant comparator material. | Argument map or evidence table. |
| Solutions and Strategies | Develop specific strategies for building emotional agility in leadership transitions Propose approaches for managing identity challenges in role transitions Create an action plan for developing psychological flexibility | Academic theory, public company or case evidence, and relevant comparator material. | Argument map or evidence table. |
| Personal Reflection | Reflect on your own emotional patterns and hooks in professional contexts Analyze how your relationship with difficult emotions could influence your leadership Discuss your personal strategies for developing emotional agility | Module theory, case incidents, and first-person reflection prompts. | Short reflective section with named hooks and counter-practices. |

## Rubric Signals

- Presentation/interpretation of the case study's profile (20%)
- Integration of academic theory and 'real-world' practice (15%)
- Depth of analysis and self-reflection (15%)
- Constructing recommendations that follow logically from your critical analysis (10%)
- Quality of the proposed actions (5%)
- Professional formatting and Harvard referencing (5%)
